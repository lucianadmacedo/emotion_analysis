Key Findings

The analysis reveals fundamentally different stressors:

International Students

Face systemic, external threats - deportation, visa status, work authorization (OPT/H1B), and immigration policy changes. Their emotional landscape is shaped by forces largely outside their control.

College Students

Face internal, performance-based pressures - GPA, professor relationships, academic workload, and financial aid. Their stress centers on individual achievement and campus life.
